# About The Backdoor

\toc

Compare to the potential outcomes framework in causal inference, which measure the causal effect with three assumptions, another framework of causal inference, namely structural causal model, exploits structural graphs to model causality. This post will give a concise description to several foundations of the structural causal model, including the *do*-operator and the backdoor adjustment.

## *do*-operator

**Intervention** is critical for causal inference since it can help us answer causal questions such as "*what if ...*". Then how to represent intervention in math? The potential outcomes framework uses $Y(t)$ to denote what the outcome could be if you were to take treatment $t$. This denotation is different with the observed outcome $Y$ since not all potential outcomes has been observed. Structural causal model adopts another different but similar denotation for intervention, i.e., *do*-operator. Specifically, $do(t)$ indicates intervention with treatment $t$. Note that, $Y(t)$ is equivalent to $Y|do(t)$, and they can be used on both individual level and population level. For example, we can write the distribution of potential outcome $Y(t)$ as:
$$
\text{P}(Y(t)=y) \triangleq \text{P}(Y=y|do(T=t)) \triangleq \text{P}(y|do(t))
$$
