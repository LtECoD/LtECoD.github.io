Chapter 1: The ladder of causation

This chapter introduce mainly the ladder of causation proposed by the author. The ladder includes three level, including association, intervetion and counterfactuals from bottom to top. The three levels has progressively more powerfule causal quries.

The first rung calls for prediction based on passive observations, of which one typical question is "what if I see ...". The author placed nowdays artificial intelligent machines on this rung, because machines are disappointly far from humanlike cognization. The author also quoted two precise and concise statements about artificial intelligence:

The filed of intelligence is bursting with microdiscoveries.

Human level intelligence or animal-level abilities?
